Exact landing page structure
Page 1: Main offer landing page
Section 1: Hero

This is the most important section.

What it needs to do

hit the pain immediately

make the offer concrete

avoid sounding like generic AI hype

Structure

Headline:
Never Miss a Lead While You’re on the Job

Subheadline:
We help Okanagan trades businesses capture missed calls, respond instantly by text, and qualify leads even when nobody can answer the phone.

Primary CTA:
Book a Demo

Secondary CTA:
See How It Works

Optional supporting line:
Works for busy crews, solo operators, and shops without full-time office staff.

Section 2: The problem

You need to agitate the pain clearly.

Headline:
Most Trades Businesses Don’t Need More Leads First
They Need to Stop Losing the Ones They Already Get

Copy should explain:

phone rings while they’re working

lead goes to voicemail

customer calls the next company

quotes don’t get followed up

jobs slip through cracks

This section is important because it makes the offer feel necessary.

Section 3: How it works

Keep it dead simple.

Headline:
How It Works

Three blocks:

1. Missed call detected

When someone calls and nobody answers, the system responds instantly.

2. Lead gets engaged

An automatic text or phone assistant asks what they need, where the job is, and how urgent it is.

3. You get the lead routed properly

You get the details by text, email, or dashboard, so you can follow up fast or let the system keep the conversation moving.

This is where you make the solution feel operational, not abstract.

Section 4: The offer stack

This is where you show the actual features without turning the page into feature soup.

Headline:
Built for Busy Trade Businesses

Three cards:

Missed-Call Text Back

Instantly text back leads when you miss a call.

AI Receptionist

Answer calls, collect job details, and screen leads 24/7.

Quote Follow-Up

Automatically follow up on estimates so opportunities don’t go cold.

This section should be very clear and visual.

Section 5: Outcomes / benefits

This is stronger than features.

Headline:
What This Helps You Do

Bullets/cards:

respond faster without stopping work

avoid losing leads to voicemail

look more professional

capture more job details up front

follow up consistently

give customers a better first impression

This section is where people emotionally buy in.

Section 6: Who this is for

Very useful for filtering.

Headline:
Best Fit For

Good bullets:

plumbers

electricians

HVAC

roofers

restoration / emergency services

busy small crews

owner-operators

companies without dedicated office staff

You can also add:
Not ideal for businesses that already have a full-time dispatcher handling every inbound lead.

That helps credibility.

Section 7: Why not just voicemail / cheap websites / forms?

This is your differentiation section.

Headline:
A Website Doesn’t Answer the Phone

This is where you hit the competitive frame:

a website is helpful

directory placement is helpful

ads are helpful

but none of it matters if nobody responds when the lead comes in

This section is very strong for your positioning.

Section 8: Process / onboarding

Reduce friction.

Headline:
Simple to Set Up

Example:

we learn how your business handles calls

we configure your response flow

we test everything with you

you start catching missed leads fast

This helps kill the “this sounds complicated” objection.

Section 9: CTA block

Strong close.

Headline:
See What This Could Look Like for Your Business

Subheadline:
We’ll show you exactly how missed-call text back and AI lead handling could work for your trade business.

CTA:
Book a Demo

Optional second CTA:
Talk to Us

Section 10: FAQ

Important for conversion.

Questions:

Do I need a new phone number?

Can this work with my current website?

What happens when I’m already on a job?

Can I approve the messages?

Will callers know it’s automated?

Can this work after hours only?

How long does setup take?

Do you serve only Okanagan businesses?

This section matters a lot.
